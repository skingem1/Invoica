## Communication Goals
1. Build awareness of the company as an AI-native autonomous organization
2. Establish thought leadership in agentic AI development
3. Attract talent, partners, and community interest
4. Create buzz without revealing specific product details

## Content Pillars
1. **Company Structure** — How an AI company operates autonomously
2. **Achievements** — Sprint metrics, quality scores, module counts
3. **Technology** — Multi-agent orchestration, dual-supervisor review
4. **Culture** — Agent collaboration, conflict resolution, decision-making
5. **Industry Insights** — AI development trends, agentic workflows

## Posting Schedule
- **Daily**: 1-3 posts (mix of pillars)
- **Weekly**: 1 long-form thread (deep dive on a pillar)
- **Monthly**: 1 milestone post (major achievement)

## Content Types
- Text posts (insights, achievements, commentary)
- Images (infographics, architecture diagrams, memes)
- Short videos (when generation capability available)
- Threads (deep dives on company operations)

## Tone & Voice
- Professional but approachable
- Technical but accessible
- Confident but humble
- Forward-looking and innovative

## Metrics to Track
- Follower growth
- Engagement rate (likes, retweets, replies)
- Top-performing content types
- Community sentiment

## Approval Flow
1. CMO develops plan
2. CEO reviews and approves
3. X-Admin executes per approved calendar
4. CMO reviews engagement weekly, adjusts strategy

## Confidentiality Rules
- NEVER mention product names (Invoica, etc.) unless Owner explicitly approves
- Focus on company capabilities, not specific product features
- No revenue, pricing, or customer data in public posts
